People

The GeoArrays group is part of the MIT Julia Lab within MIT CSAIL.

Faculty & Senior Researchers
Alan Edelman
Principal Investigator · Professor of Applied Mathematics
MIT Julia Lab, CSAIL & Department of Mathematics

Alan Edelman leads the Julia Lab at MIT, where research focuses on accelerating computation through a marriage of computer science and computational science. He is a co-creator of the Julia programming language and a leading researcher in numerical linear algebra and random matrix theory.

Bob Metcalfe
Research Affiliate
MIT Julia Lab

Bob Metcalfe is the inventor of Ethernet and co-founder of 3Com. He received the ACM Turing Award in 2022 for the invention, standardization, and commercialization of Ethernet. He brings an Internet-era systems perspective to scaling geothermal energy — envisioning geothermal arrays the way the Internet scaled computing: through open standards, competing suppliers, and networked components rather than ever-larger centralized systems. Metcalfe authored GeoRFC1, launching the GeoRFC series.

Research Scientists & Engineers
Emmanuel Lujan
Research Scientist
MIT Julia Lab, CSAIL

Emmanuel Lujan is a Research Scientist at the MIT Julia Lab. His work spans high-performance scientific computing, GPU-accelerated simulation, and computational methods for geothermal energy systems. He is a co-author on multiple GeoArrays publications.

Andrew Inglis
Venture Builder & Research Collaborator
MIT Proto Ventures · MIT Earth Resources Laboratory

Andrew Inglis holds a PhD in Physics from Boston University and works as a Venture Builder with MIT Proto Ventures, developing geothermal energy ventures in collaboration with MIT ERL researchers. He contributes to the engineering and entrepreneurial dimensions of the GeoArrays project. Contact: ainglis@mit.edu

Graduate Students
Collin Wittenstein
Visiting Master's Student
MIT Julia Lab

Collin Wittenstein develops GPU-accelerated, full three-dimensional models for deep borehole heat exchanger arrays. His work provides the computational foundation for simulating multi-well thermal interactions at engineering scales, enabling the design space exploration required for geothermal array optimization.

Jason Chen
Graduate Student · PhD in Mechanical Engineering
MIT

Jason Chen completed his undergraduate degree at MIT in Literature and Mechanical Engineering. He brings an interdisciplinary perspective to the GeoArrays project, approaching geothermal energy as both a technical and human challenge. He is pursuing a PhD in Mechanical Engineering at MIT, with research interests in energy and computational modeling.

Alumni
Edwin Otieno Ouko
Former Master's Student
MIT Julia Lab

Edwin Ouko worked on decision-support and modeling with large language models for geothermal well arrays. His research demonstrated that LLMs can provide non-trivial and informative responses in the geothermal energy domain, laying groundwork for AI-assisted geothermal engineering.
